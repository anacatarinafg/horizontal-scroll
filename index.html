Roses
A rose is either a woody perennial flowering plant of the genus Rosa (/ËˆroÊŠzÉ™/),[1] in the family Rosaceae (/roÊŠËˆzeÉªsiËËŒiË/),[1] or the flower it bears. There are over three hundred species and tens of thousands of cultivars. Their flowers vary in size and shape and are usually large and showy, in colours ranging from white through yellows and reds.
Tulips
Tulips (Tulipa) are a genus of spring-blooming perennial herbaceous bulbiferous geophytes (having bulbs as storage organs). The flowers are usually large, showy and brightly coloured, generally red, pink, yellow, or white (usually in warm colours). They often have a different coloured blotch at the base of the tepals (petals and sepals, collectively), internally.
